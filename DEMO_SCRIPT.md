# DYD — Demo Recording Script

**Voice:** half developer, half user. When you're talking about *why we built it like this*, you're the maker. When you're clicking around, you become whoever is on screen — Tomi the participant, Gabo the admin. Switch fluidly; let the camera tell the audience which one you are at any given moment.

**Tone:** natural, a little dry, a little fun. Not selling. You're walking a friend through something you built. Trail off where it feels natural. Don't punch every line.

**Pace:** conversational, ~135 wpm. Target total ~3:00–3:30. The dots and ellipses are real pauses; respect them.

Each stage is its own take. Set the URL at the top of the stage, hit record, do the moves in order. The voice-over (VO) blocks live next to the on-screen action they sit over. *Italics in stage directions are for you, not the take.*

## Recording notes

- **Pacing.** When in doubt, slow down. If a sentence wants to trail off, let it. *"…which we made up"* is the kind of line that should fade, not punch.
- **The video opener.** The captivation moment is the audience watching the Daremaster cold, with no narration on top. Resist the urge to talk over it.
- **Voice switching.** When you say "I'm the admin", actually feel like the admin for the next 30 seconds — your tone changes a little, you're a bit more matter-of-fact. When you step out to say "we built it this way because…", you're the developer again. The audience tracks the switch on its own.
- **Two beats matter most:**
  - The daremaster signal flip in Stage 3 — *"Now there's actual signal"* — pause before that line.
  - The final two sentences. Don't run them together. One full beat between *"It's the engine"* and *"Most things in this hackathon…"*
- **Retakes are cheap.** If a line goes sideways, restart that stage — don't try to splice mid-stage.
- **Stage 4 footnote.** This take stays on the admin side throughout — the winner post on the feed is public, so VO 18 covers the participant payoff without a role switch. If you decide later you want a literal Tomi-side shot, it's a small follow-up.
